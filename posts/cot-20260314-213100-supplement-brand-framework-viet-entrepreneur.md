## 2. CORE FRAMEWORKS

### Framework 1: The Permanent Desires Matrix
Every winning product connects to 1 or more of 3 universal, timeless desires:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│         3 PERMANENT DESIRES             │
├─────────────┬───────────────────────────┤
│  HEALTH     │ Body, energy, hormones,   │
│             │ longevity, appearance     │
├─────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  WEALTH     │ Money, status, security,  │
│             │ time freedom, career      │
├─────────────┼───────────────────────────┤
│  RELATIONSHIP│ Love, connection, social │
│             │ status, libido, belonging │
└─────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

**Rule:** Products hitting 2 desires simultaneously are significantly more powerful. The supplement hit Health (hormonal balance) + Relationship (libido) = double permanent desire.

---

### Framework 2: Market Sophistication Level (MSL)
Determines your advertising strategy based on how aware the market already is:

| Level | Market State | Strategy |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Brand new, nobody knows | Educate first, be the pioneer |
| 2 | Emerging, low competition | Make loud promise, claim dominance fast |
| 3 | Growing, moderate competition | Differentiate on unique mechanism |
| 4 | Saturated, high competition | Niche down, find underserved segment |
| 5 | Commoditised | Price war or exit |

**Application:** Entering at Level 2 = highest opportunity. Loud promise + strong belief = rapid scale.

---

### Framework 3: The Belief Reframing Technique
3-step persuasion formula for ads and copy:

```
Step 1: AGREE   → "You're right, [solution X] doesn't work"
Step 2: BRIDGE  → "Because they don't address [root cause]"
Step 3: REFRAME → "Our product solves it differently because [unique mechanism]"
```

This converts skeptics into believers. Works because you validate their frustration BEFORE selling.

---

### Framework 4: The 5 Awareness Stages Funnel
```
UNAWARE ──→ PROBLEM AWARE ──→ SOLUTION AWARE ──→ PRODUCT AWARE ──→ MOST AWARE
 VSSLs       Problem-agit.      Advertorials        Testimonials     Static images
 Education   Short VSSLs        Quiz funnels        Us vs. Them      Direct offers
                                                    Reviews          Discount CTA
```

**Key insight:** Large brands spend millions moving people from Unaware to Product Aware. You enter at Product/Most Aware and ride their marketing investment. Fastest, cheapest path to profitability.

---

### Framework 5: The MRR Compounding Engine
```
ACQUISITION COST
        ↓
  Customer acquired → One-time buyer (dead end)
                   → Subscriber (compounding)
                              ↓
                    Month 1: CAC > Revenue (loss)
                    Month 2: Break even
                    Month 3+: Pure profit
                    Month 6+: Compounding MRR
```

**6 MRR Fundamentals:**
1. **Churn Rate** — cancellations per month (target <10%)
2. **CAC** — cost to acquire one subscriber
3. **LTV** — total lifetime revenue per subscriber ($120-200 healthy)
4. **Revenue Forecasting** — predict next month from current cohorts
5. **Break-even ROAS** — minimum to not lose money
6. **Payback Period** — time to recover CAC (target ≤60 days)

## 4. STEP-BY-STEP IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

### For Infinity Lighting (LED Neon) — 90-Day Growth Sprint

---

**Week 1-2: Post-Mortem + Foundation**

```
□ Run a brutal audit against the 4 failure points:
  □ Customer retention: What % of customers buy again?
  □ P&L accuracy: Is current P&L truly profitable?
  □ Risk management: What happens if main supplier fails?
  □ Team gaps: What functions are bottlenecked by you personally?

□ Map your product against the Permanent Desires Matrix:
  □ Primary desire: Wealth (business looks premium, attracts customers)
  □ Secondary desire: Relationship (weddings, events, social media content)
  □ Document your Unique Mechanism: "Custom LED neon in [X days] 
    with [unique differentiator] unlike standard sign shops"

□ Identify your Market Sophistication Level in Australia:
  □ Survey 10 recent customers: "Where else did you look before buying?"
  □ Check Meta Ad Library: how many competitors are running ads?
  □ Determine if you're at Level 2 (loud promise) or Level 3 (differentiate)
```

**Week 3-4: Audience + Offer Architecture**

```
□ Define 3 core audience segments:
  □ Hospitality (bars, restaurants, cafes) → Wealth desire dominant
  □ Events/Weddings → Relationship desire dominant
  □ Personal brand / content creators → Relationship + Wealth

□ Build belief reframing copy for each segment:
  □ "Most neon sign shops take 4-6 weeks. Ours are ready in [X days] 
    because [specific reason]"
  □ "Custom doesn't have to mean expensive. Here's why..."

□ Design offer architecture:
  □ Entry offer: Small sign / custom quote widget on website
  □ Core offer: Standard custom neon (current product)
  □ High-ticket offer: Full installation + design consultation
  □ Subscription (test): Annual refresh program for hospitality clients
```

**Week 5-8: Acquisition Engine**

```
□ Meta Ads setup:
  □ Create Business Manager + backup BM
  □ Install pixel, test conversion events
  □ Build CBO campaign targeting AU only first
  □ Create 15-20 initial creatives: 
    - 10 UGC (real customers filming their signs)
    - 5 before/after installation shots
    - 5 static images with direct offer (Most Aware targeting)

□ First 2 weeks: DO NOT turn off for low ROAS
  □ You're feeding pixel data, not buying profit yet
  □ Monitor CPM trend — should decrease in week 2
  □ Identify first winning creative

□ CRO stack:
  □ Build advertorial: "How [type of business] in [city] increased 
    foot traffic by X% with one sign change"
  □ Add testimonials + social proof to product page
  □ Test quiz funnel: "Design Your Perfect Neon Sign"
```

**Week 9-12: Retention + Scale**

```
□ Implement MRR model for B2B clients:
  □ Offer "Sign Maintenance + Refresh Plan" monthly subscription
  □ Target: hospitality businesses with multiple locations
  □ Price: $99-199/month per location for maintenance + 1 refresh/year

□ Email flows (Klaviyo):
  □ Post-purchase: request UGC + cross-sell complementary signs
  □ 30-day: "Your friends would love this" referral sequence
  □ 90-day: "It's time to add a second sign" upsell

□ Track your MRR fundamentals:
  □ Set up cohort tracking (even basic spreadsheet first)
  □ Calculate current LTV vs target ($300+ for B2B clients)
```

## 5. KEY TAKEAWAYS

**1. Post-mortem before launch**
Every business failure contains the exact roadmap for what to fix. Ruthless self-assessment before building saves months of expensive mistakes.

**2. Product selection is the multiplier**
The right product (2 permanent desires + low MSL + strong belief) doesn't just sell — it almost sells itself. Wrong product = pushing rope regardless of marketing skill.

**3. MRR is not a feature — it's a different business model**
A brand doing $1.5M/month one-time sales and a brand doing $1.5M/month MRR are fundamentally different businesses. The MRR brand has 5-10x higher valuation, lower stress, and compounding growth. Every physical product business should ask: "How do I make this recurring?"

**4. Understand before outsourcing**
This is counterintuitive but proven. Brandon ran the ads himself for months before hiring. If you outsource what you don't understand, you can't manage it. You'll be managed by it.

**5. Your operations background is the real competitive advantage**
Most DTC founders fail at supply chain, P&L, and team building — the exact areas where your Engineering + Logistics background is strongest. You don't need to learn what they had to learn the hard way.

**6. Australia is an underutilised market for this playbook**
The video is US/Europe focused. Australian DTC market has lower competition, strong purchasing power, and adjacent access to NZ, Singapore, and SE Asia. Your local knowledge + logistics experience = moat.

**7. MER tells you the truth about your business**
```
MER = Total Revenue / Total Ad Spend
```
Track this weekly. It accounts for all revenue sources — email, organic, direct — not just paid ads. If MER is growing, your business is healthy even when daily ROAS fluctuates.
