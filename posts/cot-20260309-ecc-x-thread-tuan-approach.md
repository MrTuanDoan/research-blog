**1/**
Whenever a Claude Code repo blows up on GitHub, I don't just star it and forget.

I have a little system for turning "cool repo" into actual value for how I work.

Here's my honest approach 🧵

---

**2/**
First thing I do — I DON'T read the README myself.

I drop the GitHub link into OpenClaw (my AI assistant running Claude Opus 4.6) and say:

"Research this repo. Explain every main function → save as one doc. Then map it to my 3 work modes with real use cases → save as another doc. Push both to GitHub."

While I make coffee. Both docs are ready when I'm back.

---

**3/**
Why Opus specifically?

Claude Haiku is fast and cheap. Sonnet handles most things.

But for "understand a complex 65-skill repo and map it to MY context" — I want the model that actually thinks, not the one that just pattern-matches.

Cost maybe $1–2. Worth every cent.

---

**4/**
This week's repo: everything-claude-code by @affaanmustafa

50K+ stars. Anthropic hackathon winner.
Built over 10+ months of daily Claude Code use.

My first reaction before OpenClaw: "This is overwhelming. 65 skills, 40 commands, 16 agents..."

After: two clean docs, mapped to how I actually work. 👇

**5/**
I work across 3 modes depending on the day:

🔬 Research mode — exploring tech, evaluating tools
🎬 Content mode — turning findings into posts/newsletters
🤝 Connector mode — helping businesses find the right AI solutions

The key prompt I gave OpenClaw: "Map this to MY 3 modes. Real use cases only. Not what this CAN do — what I would actually USE."

---

**6/**
For research mode, OpenClaw pulled out 2 things:

**search-first skill** — forces AI to research existing work BEFORE suggesting solutions

I used to ask Claude to help me evaluate a new AI tool and it'd just... make stuff up.

This workflow makes it actually look things up first. Night and day difference.

---

**7/**
The second one blew my mind a bit:

**continuous-learning-v2** — AI observes HOW you work and turns your patterns into "instincts"

Over time, when I consistently evaluate tools the same way (features → cost → integration → scalability), it LEARNS that's my framework.

No re-explaining every session.

---

**8/**
For content mode:

**article-writing skill** is built specifically to avoid generic AI tone.

You feed it samples of YOUR writing → it matches your voice.

The difference between "AI wrote this" and "this sounds like me" is huge. Still needs editing but it's a much better starting point.

---

**9/**
**content-engine skill** is the one I wish I had 6 months ago.

One raw idea → LinkedIn post, Twitter thread, newsletter section, short-form — all formatted for each platform.

Not perfect, but it removes the "okay now I have to reformat this 4 times" friction.

---

**10/**
For connector/business mode:

**market-research skill** gives you source-attributed research.

Every claim comes with [Source: URL].

This sounds small but it's not. When you're presenting to a client, "here's the landscape with actual sources" hits different than vibes.

**12/**
My honest take after OpenClaw finished the research:

This repo is NOT a magic button.

It's more like a really well-stocked kitchen. You still need to know what you're cooking.

But the ingredients are good. The recipes are tested. And the chef clearly uses this daily.

**13/**
The meta-habit I now use with any rising-star AI repo:

① Give the link to OpenClaw with Opus
② Ask: "Explain every function + map to my context"
③ Read the output (not the whole repo)
④ Pick 2–3 things to test this week
⑤ Revisit in 30 days — still useful? Go deeper.

Total time: 15 minutes of actual thinking. The rest is AI.
