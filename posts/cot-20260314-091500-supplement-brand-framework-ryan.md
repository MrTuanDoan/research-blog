## 🧩 CONTENT BREAKDOWN — TRỌNG ĐIỂM ĐỂ HỌC

### Module 1: Product Research & Market Fit

**Permanent Desires Framework:**
Every winning product connects to 1 or 2 of 3 permanent desires:
- **Health** (body, hormones, energy, longevity)
- **Wealth** (money, status, financial security)
- **Relationships** (love, connection, libido, social status)

The menopause supplement hit: Health (hormonal balance) + Relationships (libido). Double permanent desire = strong product-market fit.

**Market Sophistication Levels:**
| Level | Description | Strategy |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Brand new market, no awareness | Educate first |
| 2 | Emerging, low competition | Louder promise, claim dominance |
| 3 | Saturated, aware market | Differentiate mechanism |
| 4-5 | Highly saturated | Niche down or re-enter with twist |

**Unique Mechanism Formula:**
Don't be "another supplement." Be "the only supplement that [mechanism] because [root cause]."
```
❌ "A menopause supplement that helps with symptoms"
✅ "The only supplement targeting hormonal balance at the root — 
    not just masking symptoms like most doctor-prescribed solutions"
```

**Belief Reframing Technique:**
1. Agree with existing belief: "You're right, most [solutions] don't work"
2. Bridge: "Because they don't target [root cause]"
3. Introduce your mechanism: "Our product does [X] differently"

### Module 2: Testing & Scaling (Facebook Ads)

**The 5 Awareness Stages:**
```
Unaware → Problem Aware → Solution Aware → Product Aware → Most Aware
```

**Key insight:** Most brands fight at "Product Aware" stage. Smarter play: target "Most Aware" (people already convinced the category works, just need to choose YOU).

**Content types by awareness:**
| Stage | Content Type |
|-------|-------------|
| Unaware | Long VSSLs, educational content |
| Problem Aware | Short VSSLs, problem-agitation |
| Solution Aware | Advertorials, quiz funnels |
| Product Aware | Testimonials, reviews, us-vs-them |
| Most Aware | Static images, discount offers, direct CTA |

**The 2-Week Rule:**
- First 2 weeks = UNPROFITABLE on purpose
- You're feeding pixel + ad account data (70% ad account, 30% pixel)
- Never turn off ads after 3 days like fashion/general dropshippers
- After data accumulates: CPM drops, CTR improves, winning creatives emerge

**Scaling Method:**
```
1. Download TikTok Shop winners (proof of concept)
2. Launch on Meta CBO, US only, all in one adset
3. Feed pixel data for 2 weeks
4. Once profitable: add new creatives DAILY
5. Test static images for most-aware stages (quick wins)
6. Launch UGC recreations with Fiverr creators ($100/creator)
7. Build own UGC engine → higher quality + no DMCA risk
```

### Module 3: CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)

**The CRO Hierarchy (test in this order):**
1. **Offer** — bundle pricing, discount structure, perceived value
2. **Advertorial** — long-form pre-sell page before product page
3. **Landing page** — above-the-fold, hero section, CTA
4. **Product page** — social proof, product description, trust signals
5. **Quiz funnel** — personalized recommendation path

**AB Testing Approach:**
Test one element at a time. Measure: CVR (conversion rate) and RPV (revenue per visitor).

**The Advertorial Formula:**
- Story-based, not sales-based
- Agitate the problem they already feel
- Introduce the mechanism (your unique angle)
- Social proof embedded in the story
- Soft CTA at the end

### Module 4: Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) — The Game Changer

This is the most important module for Ryan. This is what separates a real business from a hustle.

**MRR vs Non-MRR comparison:**

| Metric | No MRR | With MRR |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Month start | $0, start from scratch | Guaranteed recurring base |
| Cash flow | Unpredictable, daily stress | Predictable, forecastable |
| Ad strategy | Need immediate ROAS profit | Can spend ahead, renewals cover |
| LTV | $40-60 | $120-200 |
| Business valuation | 1-2x monthly profit | 5-10x monthly profit |
| Growth model | Linear (more ads = more revenue) | Compounding (subscribers stack) |

**6 MRR Fundamentals:**
1. **Churn Rate** — % of subscribers cancelling monthly (target: <10%)
2. **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost) — what you pay to acquire one subscriber
3. **LTV** (Lifetime Value) — total revenue per subscriber over lifetime
4. **Revenue Forecasting** — predict next month's revenue → plan ad spend
5. **Break-even ROAS** — the minimum ROAS to not lose money on acquisition
6. **Payback Period** — how many months until acquisition cost is recovered (target: ≤60 days)

**Subscription Implementation (4 Phases):**
```
Phase 1: Test subscriptions, expect 5-10% take rate
Phase 2: Optimize offers → reach 30-40% take rate
Phase 3: Hit 80-90% take rate → compounding power begins
Phase 4: Ongoing — test sub-offers to increase CVR and AOV
```

**MER Formula:**
MER = Total Revenue / Total Ad Spend
(More holistic than ROAS — includes all channels, email, organic)

**The Cohort Model:**
Track monthly cohorts: AOV + Ad Spend + New Customers → see compounding revenue growth over time. If you turned off all ads today, how much MRR do you still have? That number is your true business health indicator.
